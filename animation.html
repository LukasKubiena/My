We look at paintings and not just photographs, because there are emotions and stories only paint can tell.

Animation makes every frame a painting.

Yet somehow we have this erroneous notion that animation only is for kids.

I think Sayoshi Kon, Hayoa Miyazaki, Hideki Anno, have shown otherwise.

I am founder of the animation studio Orca's Lair.

To quote one of the man's himself animation is about expression. And expression is:

"basically the process of creating something that's missing,

attempting to get an idea across to someone else... trying to make yourself whole at the same time...

…for those who have a hard time relating to the people around them

It's, at the very least, kind of like being able to pass something along

to maintain a relationship between you and those people.

That's what I think expression is...

at least, for me it is…

Anime and movies are ways I am able to continue relating to others…

That's what I think."
